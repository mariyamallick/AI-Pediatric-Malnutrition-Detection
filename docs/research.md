# Pediatric Malnutrition Research

## Introduction

Pediatric malnutrition is a condition in which children do not receive adequate nutrition for healthy growth and development. It can result from insufficient food intake, poor nutrient absorption, illness, or inadequate dietary quality.

Early identification of malnutrition is essential because delayed diagnosis can lead to impaired physical growth, weakened immunity, cognitive delays, and increased risk of mortality.

This project aims to assist healthcare professionals by using Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) to identify children at risk of malnutrition and provide evidence-based nutritional recommendations.

## Types of Pediatric Malnutrition

### 1. Stunting
- Low height for age.
- Indicates chronic malnutrition.

### 2. Wasting
- Low weight for height.
- Indicates acute malnutrition.

### 3. Underweight
- Low weight for age.

### 4. Micronutrient Deficiencies
Examples include:
- Iron deficiency
- Vitamin A deficiency
- Zinc deficiency
- Vitamin D deficiency

## WHO Growth Indicators

The AI model will use anthropometric measurements such as:

- Age
- Gender
- Weight
- Height
- Body Mass Index (BMI)
- Mid Upper Arm Circumference (MUAC)
- Hemoglobin level (if available)

These measurements help determine a child's nutritional status according to WHO growth standards.

## Clinical Symptoms

Possible symptoms considered during assessment include:

- Poor weight gain
- Fatigue
- Pale skin
- Hair loss
- Reduced appetite
- Frequent infections
- Delayed growth
